PolitiBeast is a new service designed to make it easier to figure out and follow what is going on in Congress.

The two core features of PolitiBeast are its congressmen database and its bills database. With the congressmen database, you will be able to lookup any congressman's positions, call or email them right from the website or phone app, see which bills they are sponsoring or have sponsored in the past, and of course visualize their voting record. You will even have their Twitter feed available right on the page, to see what their latest thoughts are.

The bills database will allow you to read the text itself, but also see its revisions, and have direct links to other bills which it references, its sponsors, news articles related to it, and of course, once the vote has passed, the list of those who voted Yea, and who voted Nay.

At this point you might exclaim, "But... all of this data is already publically available!"

PolitiBeast's value is how it gives you access to all of this data: a rapid, responsive site that works as well on your computer as it does on your tablet or phone. Simply put: your one-stop destination for all things Capitol Hill.

Mobile apps for iOS, Android, and Windows Phone are also planned, to make it even easier to stay informed on the go. On top of this, you'll have the option to create a PolitiBeast account, where you can follow individual congressmen, bills, or even issues.

FAQ

What will it cost to use PolitiBeast?
PolitiBeast is a not-for-profit service, the website will be absolutely free to use.
How will you get the data for PolitiBeast?
The main source of data we are using for PolitiBeast is the New York Times' Congress API.
